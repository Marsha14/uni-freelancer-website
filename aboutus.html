About UNI Freelancers

We are a youth-led hub in Kibera empowering marginalized young people with digital skills and dignified work—connecting local talent with real opportunities in data entry and monitoring & evaluation.

Partner with Us Become a Member

Our Story

Growing up in Kibera, we saw brilliant young people full of potential but trapped by unemployment, poverty, and lack of opportunity. Seven transformative years at ChezaCheza Dance Foundation taught our founder the value of discipline, community, and empowerment—planting the seed for what later became UNI Freelancer.

Today, UNI Freelancers trains and engages youth in digital work—especially data entry and M&E tasks—skills that are in high demand yet often inaccessible to young people from informal settlements. We restore confidence while creating dignified income.

The Problem

Youth in Kibera lack meaningful work opportunities. Meanwhile, many organizations struggle to manage their data effectively.

Our Solution

We connect these realities by equipping youth with market-ready digital skills and delivering affordable, reliable data services for community organizations. A challenge becomes a solution.

Why UNI Freelancers

UNI Freelancers deserves recognition because it represents the voice, resilience, and potential of marginalized youth. Our model tackles unemployment by building digital capacity and connecting youth to dignified work, while supporting organizations with affordable data management.

What makes us unique: we are not just for youth—we are led by youth. That means relevance, empathy, and sustainability are built-in.

Future Vision (3–5 Years)

  • Become a recognized hub for digital work in Kibera and a replicable model for other communities.
  • Train and employ 500+ youth.
  • Expand partnerships with local and international organizations.
  • Develop a digital platform connecting youth to data entry and M&E opportunities.
  • Invest in equipment, connectivity, and a functional community workspace.
  • Strengthen training to keep pace with evolving digital skills.
  • Continuously adapt to community needs to remain inclusive and impactful.
